🧠 Study Science — How to Use This Tool

Spaced Repetition: Don't marathon one topic. Rotate through topics, revisiting older material. This exploits the "spacing effect" — one of the most replicated findings in memory research.

Active Recall: Use the Practice & Flashcard modes instead of re-reading. Retrieving info from memory strengthens it 2-3x more than passive review.

Interleaving: Mix topics together in each session. It feels harder but produces dramatically better long-term retention.

The Testing Effect: Take the practice problems before studying — even getting them wrong improves final retention by 20-40%.

🔬 Exam Day Tips (Science-Backed)

• Retrieval practice over re-reading: If you have 1 hour left, do practice problems — not re-reading slides.

• Brain dump first: When you get the exam, spend 60 seconds writing formulas from memory on scratch paper before question 1.

• Skip and return: Don't spend >3 min on any one question. Mark it and come back — other questions often trigger memory.

• Sleep is non-negotiable: Sleep consolidates memory. A well-rested brain outperforms a tired cramming session every time.
